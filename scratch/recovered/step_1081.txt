5: ## User Review Required
6: 
7: > [!IMPORTANT]
8: > **Drag & Drop Gesture & State Machine**:
9: > - We will use the **Thumb + Index Pinch Hold** gesture.
10: > - To avoid accidental clicks/drags, we implement a **Configurable Hold Threshold** (default: `300ms`).
11: > - **State Machine**:
12: >   - On Pinch: Enter `PINCH_PREP` state and record timestamp.
13: >   - If released before `300ms`: Trigger a single `pyautogui.click()` (standard Left Click).
14: >   - If held $\ge$ `300ms`: Trigger `pyautogui.mouseDown()` and transition to the `DRAGGING` state.
15: >   - While in `DRAGGING` state: The cursor continues to move (dragging target). Visual feed overlay displays **`DRAGGING`** feedback.
16: >   - On Release: Trigger `pyautogui.mouseUp()` (drop target) and transition back to `OPEN`/`READY`.
17: >
18: > **Safety (ESC Key)**: Pressing ESC will immediately call `pyautogui.mouseUp()` in addition to disabling the virtual mouse to ensure the mouse cursor never gets stuck in a dragging state.
